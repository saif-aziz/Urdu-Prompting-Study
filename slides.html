Motivation

A Practical Question Nobody Had Answered

The Reality

Most NLP practitioners can't afford GPT-4. They use small open-source models (0.5B – 2B) that fit on a free Colab GPU.

The Dilemma

When building a Urdu application, should the prompt be in Urdu (matching the task) or English (matching the training data)?

The Gap

Prior work (MEGA, ChatGPT Beyond English) only studied 7B+ models. The small-model regime for Urdu was untested.

Our Answer

A controlled experiment: 6 prompt variants × 2 models, paired significance tests, automatic error bucketing.

Research Question

"Does prompting in Urdu or English yield better performance for Urdu-native tasks on small multilingual language models, and does the answer depend on the prompting regime?"

Discussion

Why English Wins — Two Mechanisms

① Semantic instruction quality

At zero-shot and few-shot level, unknown rate = 0% for both languages. The model isn't refusing Urdu — it's collapsing labels. This means the failure is about understanding the class boundaries, not following the instruction format. The model grasps "negative/neutral/positive" better in English.

② Instruction-following failure (CoT)

At chain-of-thought level, Urdu adds a second failure mode: the model can't reason in Urdu. It echoes the prompt back or produces garbage, raising refusals to 23.1%. English CoT at least produces structured reasoning even when the final label is wrong.

③ Model size doesn't close the gap

The 1.5B model widens the EN–UR gap at few-shot (Δ=0.237 vs Δ=0.040 for 0.5B). Larger models exploit English demonstrations better — they don't get better at Urdu instructions.

④ Prompting beats fine-tuning here

Best prompted cell (0.526) beats fine-tuned XLM-RoBERTa (0.306). Caveat: our baseline used only 3 epochs, no hyperparameter search. A properly tuned fine-tuned model would likely recover.

Conclusion

The Answer Is Clear

Main finding

English prompts consistently outperform Urdu prompts across both models and all three regimes. 4 of 6 McNemar tests are significant, all in English's favour. The largest gap is Qwen 1.5B few-shot: 0.526 EN vs 0.289 UR.

Root cause

Label collapse under Urdu zero-shot/few-shot (predicts one class for everything) and elevated refusals under Urdu CoT (23.1%). Sub-2B models process Urdu system turns unreliably.

Practical recommendation

Use English instruction prompts with Roman-Urdu input text for small instruct LMs — until models in this range receive substantially more Urdu instruction-tuning data.

Future work: Nastaliq-script inputs · 7B+ models · parameter-efficient fine-tuning · XLT hybrid prompts
